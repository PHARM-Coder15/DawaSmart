b) Why was 1200mg stat given? For rapid clearance of infection , to attain maximum therapeutic effect.

Prednisolone Tapering

Rx: Mr. Chandika presents with a prescription of RX prednisolone 200mg bid for the first week, the 10mg bid for the second week following by 5mg bid for one week.

a) Name of this approach: Dose tapering

b) Why done? To avoid withdrawal syndrome (fatigue, headache) and prevent immunosuppression from prolonged use.

Clinical Pearl: Corticosteroids must never be stopped abruptly after prolonged use always taper to allow adrenal recovery.

5. Drug Interactions & Contraindications Explained

Why aminoglycoside antibiotics are not used by oral route?

is Poorly absorbed in GIT due to polarity and large molecular size.

Why insulin is not used by oral route?

is Protein in nature would be digested by enzymes in stomach.

Why Spiro lactone combined with furosemide?

Furosemide causes potassium loss; spironolactone spares potassium hense balances electrolytes.

Why aluminum and magnesium combined in antacids?

Magnesium causes diarrhea, aluminum causes constipation so they combined to balance colonic effects.

why is not advice to administer paracetamol together with alcohol?

Both metabolized in liver; can cause liver toxicity/damage.

Why is not advice to use alcohol when administering metronidazole.

May leads to Disulfiram-like reaction (flushing, nausea, vomiting).

Why Pregabalin is advised to be administered at night

Because of its side effects of severe sedation, dizziness and drowses

why is not advice to administer ciprofloxacin together with milk.

Because milk can lower absorption of ciprofloxacin in the body , due to formation of complex compound with minerals such as calcium found in the milk.

Why is not advice to take antacids together with milk.

may Forms complex compound hence no absorption of Calcium found in milk.

Why is not advice to administer iron supplements together with antiacid.

Because administration of iron supplements with ant acid makes formation of complex compound hence no absorption of iron supplements.

Why Aspirin is contraindication in pregnant women near birth?

Due to risk of bleeding complication near the birth.

Why there is no aspirin suspension in the market

Because aspirin is acidic in nature make it unstable in liquid form..

What type of foods are to be taken by a person who is using Griseofulvin.

Fatty meals - since it facilitates its absorption of griseofulvin because its lipophilic in nature.

In management of Parkinson disease, Levodopa is co-administered with carbidopa. Why?

Carbidopa prevents peripheral conversion of Levodopa to dopamine,hence reducing side effects and allowing more to reach CNS.

Why verapamil contraindicated in heart failure?

Because verapamil is calcium channel blocker which block the movement of calcium ions in the cardiac muscles thus depresses cardiac contractility.

Why is advice to administer Lumefantrine with high fat meal?

To increase absorption of drug, since lumefantrine its lipophilic drug in nature.

  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Joint pains
  • Malaise
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhoea
  • Body ache, body weakness
  • Poor appetite
  • Pallor, enlarged spleen

Pharmacological Treatment of Uncomplicated Malaria

  • Artemether+Lumefantrine (FDC) (PO) 20mg+120mg
  • Dihydroartemisinin + Piperaquine (PO) 40 mg+ 320 mg (Duo-Cotexin)

Severe Malaria Clinical presentation

  • extreme weakness
  • Convulsions
  • Respiratory distress
  • Impaired consciousness
  • Jaundice
  • Vomiting everything
  • Inability to drink or breast feed
  • Bleeding tendency

Pharmacological Treatment of Severe Malaria

Parenteral artesunate


@Dosage ADULT;2.4 mg/kg in body weight. (IV) or (IM) given on admission (time = 0 hour), then at 12 hours and 24 hours for a minimum of 3 injections in 24 hours regardless of patient's recovery.

@Dosage CHILD;Children weighing less than 20 kg Dosage: 3 mg/kg/dose (or higher). Same schedule as indicated above (0, 12, 24 hours)

Altenative; Injectable Artemether


@Loading dose; 3.2mg/kg IM stat (0hour)

@Maintanance dose; then 1.6mg/kg (24hours and 48hours).

Malaria in Pregnancy

  • Artemether/Lumefantrine (ALu) is the recommended treatment of choice of a confirmed uncomplicated malaria to pregnant women in all trimesters.
  • Severe Malaria in Pregnancy; Pharmacological Treatment Intramuscular/ intravenous Artesunate is the drug of choice for treatment of severe malaria in all trimesters.

Intermittent Preventive Treatment in Pregnancy (IPTp) (Prophylaxis)

  1. medicine of choice for IPTp; sulphadoxine + pyrimethamine (FDC) (PO) 500mg +25mg
  2. The dose is 3 tablets once
  3. A minimum of 3 doses in entire pregnancy period
  4. The first dose should be administered from 14 weeks of pregnancy onwards
  5. Each dose should be given at least 4 weeks apart
  6. SP should not be administered to women receiving cotrimoxazole prophylaxis or pregnant women who are taking folic acid at a daily dose equal or above 5 mg, as it counteracts its efficacy

22. Metered Dose Inhaler (MDI) Technique

What is an aerosol?

What are the advantages of aerosol dosage forms?

  • easily and quickly administration
  • A dose can be removed without contamination of materials
  • Stability is enhanced
  • Rapid onset of action
  • medication can be delivered directly to the affected area

  1. Remove cap, shake well
  2. Breathe out fully
  3. Place mouthpiece between teeth, close lips around it
  4. Take deep breath while pressing inhaler
  5. Hold breath for 10 seconds
  6. Breathe out slowly

  • Return to donor/manufacturer
  • Burning in open container
  • Encapsulation
  • Inertization
  • Incineration

Steps for handling expired(Unfit) medicine in community pharmacy

  1. Maintain register book for unfit products
  2. Keep in different categories by dosage form
  3. Keep separately controlled drugs, antineoplastics, antibiotics, hazardous medicines
  4. Keep in containers according to dosage form
  5. Demarcate area for storing these container and labeled "Expired medicines Not for Sale" in red ink
  6. Maintain safe custody until disposal
  7. Decision of when to initiate disposal shall be made by regional ,district or institution pharmacist others are owner,incharge,dispenser,inpectors
