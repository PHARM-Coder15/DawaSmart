1. Key Definitions (Pharmacy Act 2011, Cap 311)

Pharmaceutical personnel
means Registered pharmacist, enrolled pharmaceutical technician, enlisted pharmaceutical assistant, and intern pharmacist.
Pharmacist
A Person registered under section 16 of the Pharmacy Act 2011.
Intern Pharmacist
A Person registered under section 17 of the Pharmacy Act 2011.
Pharmaceutical technician
A Person enrolled under section 24 of the Pharmacy Act 2011.
Pharmaceutical assistant
A Person enlisted under section 28 of the Pharmacy Act 2011.
Registered person
Includes registered pharmacist, enrolled pharmaceutical technicians, and enlisted pharmaceutical assistants.
Professional misconduct
is the breach of the code of conduct prescribed under the Act.
Pharmacy
Approved premises where pharmacy practice is carried out, including community, consultant, institutional, or wholesale pharmacy.
Pharmacy practice
Any acts pertaining to the scope of practice of a pharmacist, pharmaceutical technician, or pharmaceutical assistant as approved by the Council.
Business of a pharmacy
Includes professional pharmacy practice and any activity related to medicines, medical devices, or herbal medicines.
Retail pharmacy business
Retail sale of medicines, but does not include professional practice by a medical practitioner or veterinarian.
Wholesale Pharmacy
Is the Pharmacy that buys medicines in bulk and sells to retail pharmacies.
Manufacturing pharmacy
Pharmacy that manufactures medicines and sells to wholesale, community, institutional pharmacy, or authorised persons.
Community pharmacy
Business of a pharmacy located in a community , selling pharmaceuticals at retail.
Consultant pharmacy
Pharmacy providing specialised pharmaceutical services.
Institutional pharmacy
Pharmacy situated in a health facility registered by the Minister.
Prescription
Lawful written direction by a medical practitioner for preparation and dispensation of a medicine.
Superintendent
Pharmacist in-charge who supervises a pharmacy and is registered as such.
Prescription medicine
Medicine required to be dispensed only upon a prescription.
Register/Roll/List
Maintained by the Council for pharmacists, technicians, and assistants respectively.
Registrar
Means a Registrar of the Council appointed under section 12.
Council
Means Pharmacy Council established under Section 3 of the Pharmacy Act 2011.
Breaches
Act of breaking, violating, or disregarding any obligation of the code of ethics.
Controlled drug
Any narcotic drug, psychotropic substance, or precursor as listed under section 77.
Inspector
An Inspector appointed or recognised under the Act.
Premises
Includes land, building, structure, vessel, or place of storage used in connection with pharmacy practice.

2. Rational & Irrational Use of Medicine

Definition of Rational Use of Medicine

drugs requires that Patients receive medications appropriate to their clinical needs, in doses that meet their own individual requirements, for an adequate period of time, and at the lowest cost to them and their community.

Mention (5) Components of Rational Use(March 2026)

  • Availability of essential medicines
  • Access and affordability
  • Rationality of prescription

Importance of Rational Use

  • Reducing drug resistance
  • Improving patient adherence
  • Reducing unnecessary cost
  • Reducing treatment failure
  • Reducing adverse drug reactions

Criteria for Assessing Rational Use

  • Appropriate dose
  • Appropriate indication
  • Appropriate drug
  • Correct dosage
  • Appropriate patient
  • Correctly dispensed
  • Adherence

Roles of Pharmaceutical Personnel in Promoting Rational use of Medicine (Essay Feb 2025)

  • Patient medication counselling
  • Specialist in preventing drug abuse
  • Role in self-care and self-medication
  • Combating counterfeit drugs
  • Pharmaceutical care
  • Policy perspective changes
  • Pharmaceutical public health
  • Research and communication

Irrational Use of Medicine

Use of medicines in a way that is not compliant with rational use-overuse, underuse, misuse.

Based on Prescribing:

  • Under-prescribing
  • Over-prescribing
  • Incorrect prescribing
  • Extravagant prescribing
  • Multiple prescribing
  • Poly-pharmacy

Dispensing characteristics leading to irrational use:

  • Incorrect interpretation of prescription
  • Wrong medicines/ingredients
  • Inaccurate counting, compounding
  • Inadequate labelling
  • Unsanitary procedures
  • Poor quality packaging

Factors Contributing to Irrational Use

  • Lack of skills and knowledge
  • Unethical promotion
  • Overworked of health workers
  • Lack of coordinated policy
  • Poor communication
  • Inadequate supervision
  • Misleading beliefs
  • Irrational self-medication

Effects/impact caused by irrational use of medicine(FEB 2025 QN)

  • Antimicrobial resistance
  • Adverse drug reactions
  • Lost resources
  • Eroded patient confidence
  • Treatment failure
  • Increased toxicity risk
  • Non-compliance

Control measures against irrational use of medicine

  • Educational strategies
  • Printed materials
  • Face-to-face contact
  • Managerial strategies
  • Prescribing and dispensing approaches
  • Financing
  • Regulatory strategies

The following are twelve core national interventions to promote rational use of medicines

  1. Multi-disciplinary national body
  2. Clinical guidelines
  3. Essential medicines list
  4. Drugs and therapeutics committees
  5. Problem-based pharmacotherapy training
  6. Continuing in-service education
  7. Supervision, audit, feedback
  8. Independent information on medicines
  9. Public education
  10. Avoidance of perverse incentives
  11. Appropriate and enforced regulation
  12. Sufficient government expenditure

3. Counterfeit, Substandard & Unfit Medicines

Definitions

Counterfeit medicines:Are Deliberately and fraudulently mislabeled medicine

Substandard medicine: Any branded or generic drug that does not meet national or international standards.

Prohibited drugs:
means any drug declared to be a prohibited drug in terms of the provisions of section 81 of this Act (TFDC Act)
Expired:
means a product should not be sold because of an expected decline in quality or effectiveness after a certain date.
Adulterated drug product
is a drug product whose composition has been affected by the addition thereto or subtraction of any substance

Medicines and Cosmetic products shall be considered as unfit when they are:

  • Expired.
  • improperly sealed.
  • damaged, unexpired and improperly stored.
  • improperly labelled.
  • counterfeit, substandard and adulterated.
  • Prohibited
  • Unauthorized.

Forms in which substandard and counterfeit occurs

  • No active ingredients
  • Toxic ingredients
  • Wrong ingredients but therapeutically active
  • Correct drug and quantities but fake packaging
  • Incorrect quantities of API
  • High level of impurities
  • Correct drug but not registered brand (copies)

List three characteristics that could assist in identification of counterfeit medicines in the pharmacy.

  • Visual inspection of package and dosage form
  • Source
  • Price
  • Unexpected side effects

Others Chromatographic Techniques , Use of Cutting Edge Technologies , Melting-point determination

What are the characteristics of counterfeit medicine.

  • Manufactured under a name belonging to another drug
  • Label bears fictitious manufacturer name
  • Substituted wholly or partly by another substance
  • Purports to be a product of a manufacturer it is not

8. HANDLING OF CONTROLLED DRUGS

Definitions

Controlled drug/medicine: Any narcotic drug, psychotropic substance, or precursor listed under Section 77.

Narcotic drugs: Substances referred to in Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs 1961 (e.g., methadone, cocaine, pethidine, morphine, heroin, codeine).

Psychotropic substances: Substances referred to in Convention on Psychotropic Substances 1971 (e.g., amphetamine, methamphetamine, phenobarbital, diazepam, lorazepam, clonazepam).

Categories of Controlled Drugs(March 2026)

  • Narcotic drugs
  • Psychotropic substances
  • precursor

Categories of Human and Veterinary Medicines

  • Prescription only medicine
  • Pharmacy only medicine
  • General sales medicine
  • ADDO prescription medicine
  • Controlled medicine

Classification of Controlled Substances (Schedules)

  • Schedule I: No accepted medical use, high abuse potential (e.g., marijuana, heroin, cathinone).
  • Schedule II: High abuse potential, severe dependence, accepted medical use (e.g., cocaine, morphine, methadone).
  • Schedule III: Moderate to low dependence, accepted medical use (e.g., buprenorphine, dihydrocodeine, ketamine).
  • Schedule IV: Low abuse potential, low dependence (e.g., diazepam, clonazepam, lorazepam).

Restrictions for Storing Controlled Substances(Requirement for Storing)

  • Do not share controlled substance storage facilities..
  • Do not store controlled substance in a corridor.
  • Do not store other chemicals or supplies in a controlled substance storage unit.
  • Do not transfer controlled substance from its original container for storage purposes.
  • Do not store keys in unlocked drawer or hanging on the wall.
  • Use storage areas only for controlled substances and inventory log book(s).
  • Keep keys in physical custody of Authorized Personnel.
  • Plan to allow access to locked area in case of unannounced inspection/audit.

Requirements of Premises for Controlled Substances

  • Should have registered pharmaceutical personnel or any other authorized personnel.
  • Have Register/ledger for entry and issuance.
  • Storage areas designed for good storage conditions.
  • Drugs shall Kept under lock and key; key kept by pharmacist in charge.
  • Clean, free from accumulated waste and vermin.
  • Narcotics recorded and stored in controlled drugs box/room inside main pharmacy.
  • Damaged/empty/expired controlled drugs should returned to pharmacist in charge for authentication and recorded.
  • Sufficient capacity for orderly storage.

9. PRESCRIPTION HANDLING AND CONTROL REGULATION 2020.

Prescription

Lawful written direction for preparation and dispensing of a drug.

Mention Appropriate practitioner legible to write prescription.:

  • Medical practitioner
  • Dentist
  • Veterinarian
  • Any other person authorised by minister

Types of Prescription

  • Electronic prescription
  • Manual prescription

Prescription at various level of health facilities:

  • White -tertiary level
  • Yellow - secondary level
  • Blue - primary level
  • Any other as determined

Mention Essential Particulars on Prescription

@Note! ; If prescription missing this particulars is Invalid

  • Serial number
  • Name, level, registration number, address of health facility
  • Day, month, year of generation
  • Name, age, sex, body weight, physical address of patient
  • Disease code (where applicable)
  • Generic name of medicine, dosage form, strength, dosage
  • Prescriber name, designation, registration, telephone, signature, stamp
  • Period of supply, refill (if any)

Validity of Prescription

  • Normal prescription:shall be valid for a period 7 days from date of generation.
  • Prescription with refills shall be valid for one hundred and eighty days (180)
  • Refills not allowed for controlled drugs.

Dispensing of prescription

@Note! ; This are the key issues to be observed during dispensing of prescription

  • Authenticate prescription and prescriber validity.
  • Adhere to good dispensing procedures.
  • Do not dispense illegible or invalid prescriptions.
  • Consult prescriber for discrepancies, correction, adjustment.
  • Ensure prescription dully filled and signed by all parties.
  • Retain original and second copy; give third copy to patient.

Prescriptions for Controlled Drugs

  • Contain only one type of drug.
  • Completely written in prescriber's handwriting.
  • Quantity should be written in both words and figures.
  • Counter signed by patient or bonafide representative during dispensing.
  • Prperly Handled based on Enforcement Act and TMDA Act.

within twenty-four hours after the medicine has been supplied or dispensed, be entered in a book used regularly for the purpose, and which shall be called the Prescription Book-

  • Date on which medicine was dispensed.
  • Name, strength, dosage form, quantity supplied, batch number.
  • Name and address of person supplied.
  • Name, address, registration number of prescriber.
  • Name and address of prescribing health facility and prescription number.

Adjustment of prescription.


Adjustment of Prescription is only allowed for purposes of:

  • Improving adherence
  • Managing drug interactions
  • Preventing serious damage to health
  • Ensuring correct dosage based on clinical parameters

  • Exception; change or remove of medication, when necessary, within the health facility only

11. Disposal of Unfit Medicines and Cosmetics

PROCEDURES FOR HANDLING OF UNFIT MEDICINES AND COSMETIC PRODUCTS AT FACILITY LEVEL

  1. Maintain a register book (Annex I) for unfit
  2. Keep them into different categories by dosage form
  3. Keep separately controlled drugs, antineoplastics, antibiotics, hazardous medicines.
  4. Keep in containers according to their dosage forms to facilitate verification exercise, sorting and selection of disposal method.
  5. Demarcate an area for keeping containers of unfit medicines ; should labeled "Expired medicines, Not for Sale" in red ink.
  6. Maintain safe custody until disposal.
  7. The decision of when to initiate disposal of unfit medicines and cosmetic products shall be made by regional, district or hospital pharmacist, owners, incharge of facility, dispensers, and inspectors
  8. Application for disposal from Government institutions must be accompanied by Accountant General's approval.

Describe procedures of verification exercise of unfit pharmaceutical products

  • After verification exercise is completed, a verification form (Annex III) shall be filled and signed by both parties.
  • Verification process shall involve the following stages:
    1. Identification of the product.
    2. Separate medicines which fall under controlled drugs, antineoplastics, antibiotics and any other hazardous medicinal or cosmetic products.
    3. Sort according to Destruction Method (Annex IV).
  • After verification the applicant shall be informed by either TMDA-HQ
  • The cost of destruction shall be borne by the owner of the product as stipulated under Section 99 of Tanzania Food, Drugs and Cosmetics Act of 2003

Procedures for Destruction of Unfit Medicines and Cosmetic Products

  1. A Drug Inspector, Health Officer, Environmental Officer and Policeman shall supervise the transport of consignment from the owner's premises to the disposal site for destruction exercise.
  2. The destruction exercise shall be supervised by Health Officer, Environmental Officer, Policeman and Drug Inspector.
  3. Unfit medicines and cosmetic products shall be transported in a closed motor vehicle to avoid pilferage.
  4. Supervisors shall wear protective gears such as overalls, gloves, masks, caps and boots during the exercise.
  5. Upon completion of the exercise, a Drug Disposal Form (Annex V) shall be duly filled in and signed by the supervisors and owner/owner's representative.
  6. Drug Disposal Form shall be sent to TFDA headquarter offices.
  7. Once TFDA has received the form, a certificate of destruction of unfit medicines and cosmetic products (Annex VI) shall be prepared and sent to the consignee.
  8. Particular care shall be taken while handling anti-cancer drugs, narcotic drugs and penicillins to avoid associated hazards.

Effects of improper disposal of unfit medicine and cosmetics products.

  • Increase Morbidity and mortality
  • Diversion of expired drugs in the market

14. REGISTRATION and ADVERTISEMENT OF PHARMACEUTICAL PRODUCTS.

Requirements/condition for registration of pharmaceutical products

  • The availability of that drug is in the public interest.
  • It is safe, efficacious and of acceptable quality.
  • The premises and manufacturing operation complies with the current Good Manufacturing Practices requirements
  • It complies with any other requirements as may be prescribed by the Authority.

Condition that may lead to grant permission for importation of new pharmaceutical products in Tanzania

  • Compliance with regulatory authority
  • Submission of required documents
  • Ensure that products are properly labelled and packed
  • Payment of fees and taxes

Restriction on food, drugs, medical devices or herbal drugs advertisement

  • No person shall advertise any product regulated under this Act in a manner that is false, misleading or deceptive value, quantity, composition, merit, safety or efficacy
  • No person shall carry out any promotion activities except and after getting a written approval from the Authority
  • No person shall advertise or sell by retail any food, drugs, medical devices or herbal drugs in connection with any bonus, offer or discount.
  • Any person who not Adhere , will commits an offence

@Individual: fine of not less than one hundred thousand shillings or to imprisonment for a term of not less than two weeks or to both


@ body corporate or association: fine of not less than one million shillings

criteria for a product to be promoted

  • products should be registered and authorized by regulatory authority
  • Drugs to be promoted should be essential on sense of quality, efficacy, safety and affordable to the public.
  • No drugs should be promoted if the information and side effects of drugs is unknown.
  • Drugs promoted needs to care on patient benefits first before owner benefits.

Importance / Reason of regulating promotion and advertisement of pharmaceutical products.

  • To ensure quality of pharmaceutical products.
  • To avoid spread of counterfeit and substandard products.
  • To achieve rational use of medicine.
  • To avoid health harm that may be caused due to spread of poor-quality products.
  • To ensure there is no pharmaceutical products are promoted in false manner.

15. CONCEPT OF ESSENTIAL MEDICINE & ESSENTIAL MEDICINE LIST.

Essential medicines:

are those that satisfy the priority health care needs of the population.

Essential medicine list:

Is the list of medicines that satisfy priority health care needs of majority population.

Importance of essential medicine list.

  • Easier procurement, storage, and distribution
  • Better quality assurance
  • Training more focused and therefore simpler
  • More experience with fewer medicines
  • Non availability of irrational treatment alternatives
  • Reduction of antimicrobial resistance
  • Focused drug information
  • Better recognition of adverse drug reactions
  • Reduced confusion and increased adherence to treatment
  • Lower prices, more competition

Problems of national essential medicine list

  • Range of diseases covered by the Model List is not clear
  • Discrepancies between Model List and treatment guidelines
  • Selection is more consensus-based than evidence-based
  • Use of data on cost and cost-effectiveness unclear
  • Reasons for selection is insufficiently recorded
  • Drugs included without pharmacopoeia standard or supplier
  • Official report comes out too late, and in English only

The following are criteria for selection of medicines and medical supplies; for the facility

  • Possibilities for local manufacturer
  • Availability as single compounds
  • Stable during storage and transport
  • Relevance to the pattern of prevalent diseases in that area
  • Cost effective
  • Proven efficacy and safety
  • The knowledge and skills of the healthcare worker to administer
  • Medicine that satisfies the healthcare needs of the majority of the population
  • Assured quality and bioavailability
